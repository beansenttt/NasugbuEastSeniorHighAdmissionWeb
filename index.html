ABM

The ABM Strand stands for Accountancy, Business and Management. It's a program in senior high school designed for students interested in careers related to business, finance, or entrepreneurship. The curriculum focuses on providing a strong foundation in these areas, equipping students with the knowledge and skills they need to succeed in college and future professions.

Core Business Subjects: You'll learn about the basic principles of business and management, including marketing, finance, accounting, information technology, and entrepreneurship. In preparation for college, ABM strand offered various subjects exclusively for jobs that has a relation to Accountancy, and Business Management, and these are:

• Applied Economics
• Business Ethics and Social Responsibility
• Fundamentals of Accountancy, Business and Management 1
• Fundamentals of Accountancy, Business and Management 2
• Business Math
• Business Finance
• Organization and Management
• Principles of Marketing
• Work Immersion/Research/Career Advocacy/Culminating Activity i.e. Business Enterprise Simulation

Developing Business Skills: The program goes beyond theory, aiming to develop practical skills like communication, problem-solving, critical thinking, and crisis management. These skills are essential for success in any business environment.

Preparing for College and Careers: The ABM Strand prepares you for college degrees in business administration, accounting, marketing, finance, or entrepreneurship. It can also give you a head start if you plan to pursue a career in business or start your own business. The courses you can take to college after taking ABM strand are the following:

• BS in Accountancy
• BS in Tourism Management
• BS in Marketing Management
• BS in Operation Management
• BS in Human Resource Management
• BS in Economics
• BS in Entrepreneurship

TVL Track

TVL

The Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) Track in Senior High School (SHS) is an educational pathway that provides students with specialized skills and knowledge in various technical, vocational, and livelihood fields. It offers practical training and hands-on experience to prepare students for immediate employment or further education in specific industries or trades. The TVL Track aims to equip students with the necessary competencies to succeed in the workforce or pursue entrepreneurship opportunities in fields such as agriculture, home economics, industrial trades, and information and communications technology.

The TVL Track has six strands which are the Computer Programming, Animation, Cookery, Events and Management, Commercial Cooking, and Food and Beverages Services.

1. Computer Programming: It involves learning how to write, test, and debug code to create software applications. Students may learn programming languages like Python, Java, C++, or others, and develop skills in problem-solving, logical thinking, and algorithmic design. This track often prepares students for careers in software development, IT, and related fields.

2. Animation: It involves learning the skills and techniques needed to create animated content. This can include 2D animation, 3D animation, character animation, motion graphics, and visual effects. Students may learn software tools such as Adobe Animate, Autodesk Maya, Blender, or others to create animations for various purposes like films, television shows, video games, advertisements, and online content. The focus is on developing artistic and technical skills required to bring characters, scenes, and stories to life through animation.

3. Cookery: It involves learning culinary skills and techniques necessary for food preparation and cooking. Students typically learn about food safety and sanitation, knife skills, cooking methods (such as baking, frying, grilling, etc.), menu planning, and food presentation. The focus is on developing practical skills that prepare students for careers in the culinary industry, such as chefs, cooks, caterers, or restaurant managers. Additionally, students may also learn about nutrition, kitchen management, and entrepreneurship within the context of cookery.

4. Events and Management: It involves learning the skills necessary to plan, organize, and execute various types of events. This can include conferences, seminars, weddings, corporate events, concerts, and more. Students learn about event planning and coordination, budgeting, marketing and promotion, logistics management, vendor negotiation, and customer service. The focus is on developing both the practical and organizational skills needed to successfully manage events, whether it be in the hospitality industry, entertainment sector, or other fields where events play a significant role.

5. Commercial Cooking: It refers to the specialized training in culinary skills and techniques geared towards preparing food in a commercial or professional kitchen setting. This program focuses on equipping students with the practical skills needed to work in various food service establishments such as restaurants, hotels, catering companies, and cafeterias.

Students learn about food safety and sanitation standards, kitchen organization, menu planning, recipe execution, portion control, and presentation. They also gain hands-on experience in using kitchen equipment, mastering cooking methods, and developing a repertoire of recipes suitable for commercial settings.

Overall, commercial cooking programs in the TVL track aim to prepare students for careers as professional chefs, cooks, or culinary entrepreneurs by providing them with the necessary knowledge and skills to excel in the food service industry.

6. Food and Beverges Services: It refers to a program that focuses on the practical skills and knowledge related to the hospitality and food service industry.
